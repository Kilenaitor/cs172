Operation Education — Providing the Opportunity for a College Degree

Operation Education at the University of California, Riverside, provides assistance to United States military veterans who have sustained a severe and permanent injury while serving our nation since September 11, 2001. Recipients must meet the requirement of the University of California, Riverside for admission as a degree-seeking student.

Based on a successful program created by Dr. Karen White at the University of Idaho in 2006, Operation Education seeks to support those who have bravely served us by providing them with the means to pursue a college degree and start the next phase of their lives.

What Does Operation Education Provide?

Academic Support

Services provided to students may include:

  • Supplemental tutoring
  • Adaptive equipment, including computer hardware and voice recognition software
  • Classroom and testing accommodations
  • Disability management counseling

The program also offers assistance through mentoring, internships and job placement services.

Financial Support

Operation Education provides scholarship assistance to students. This financial assistance may include:

  • Tuition
  • Fees
  • Books
  • Living expenses

Home and Social Support

Operation Education may include additional support outside of the classroom, such as:

  • Housing and transportation
  • Childcare
  • Personal and family counseling services
