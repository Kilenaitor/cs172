Our Campus

UCR transforms lives. The university has a reputation as a place where knowledge-seekers can thrive, including students who are the first in their families to go to college and graduate students who are well into defining their life's work. UCR faculty provide the launching pad for another generation of leaders - people who will make a difference not just in the region, but in the world.

At UCR, we offer our faculty:

  • State-of-the art facilities, laboratories and offices.
  • Proximity to Los Angeles, San Diego and Orange county's metropolitan areas at a much lower cost of living.
  • Multitudes of support services.
  • A location close to recreation areas in the mountains, the desert and the beach.

The university exists as a living laboratory where scholars can delve deep into issues facing California and the world. From issues like clean air and water and safe and secure food supplies, to the development of groundbreaking technologies are all woven into the fabric of the UCR learning experience.

Our Research

UCR's innovative discoveries carry with them the economic, scientific and social impacts that make the rest of the world stand up and take notice. UCR is a place where dedicated researchers get the support and resources they need to make great strides.

Even with those lofty goals, UCR remains accessible to students seeking an excellent education from dedicated and respected faculty. It provides an affordable option to faculty members who want to continue their work in California, with close access to the cultural amenities of major metropolitan areas nearby.

Our People

Our 850 faculty members run the gamut from established professors in the prime of their research to up-and-coming academics who are bringing exciting new ideas to established fields of study.

UCR's faculty are known throughout the world as experts in their fields of study. Seven current and former faculty members have been elected to the National Academy of Sciences, which honors distinguished scholars for their work to advance science and technology and for helping society as a whole.

Guggenheim Fellowships, grants that help provide researchers with time - from six months to a year -- in which they can explore their chosen discipline in depth, have been awarded to more than 50 UCR faculty members.

More than 100 UCR faculty members have been elected fellows of the American Association for the Advancement of Science, a recognition of how these faculty members have made notable contributions to the advancement of science and its role in society.
