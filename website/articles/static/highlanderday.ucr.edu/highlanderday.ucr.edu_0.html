Highlander Day 2017 FAQs

Do I have to make a reservation?

Yes. Please let us know that you will be attending and how many guests you will be bringing with you. You can provide your reservation through the online form as soon as it becomes available.

Do I have to show up right at 10:00 a.m. to check in?

While it's not a requirement for you to show up at 10:00 a.m., please keep in mind that the first session will begin promptly at 10:00 a.m. and most sessions are not repeated. Your best bet is to take a look at the agenda for Highlander Day as soon as it becomes available and plan your day from there

Highlander Day will go on rain or shine. All presentations listed on the agenda are conducted indoors. Safety of our guests and UCR personnel is most important so outdoor programing may change or be cancelled last minute due to weather conditions.

Is there a cost to attend?
Nope. It’s on the house.
Is there a charge for parking?
No, parking is free.
Where do I park?
Parking Lot 30.
Once I park, where do I go?
We’ll have signs directing you. Follow the crowd.
What if I need to change the number of people I reserved space for/cancel my reservation?
There's no need to change the number of people or cancel your reservation. We take RSVP's for general planning purposes. If you bring more or fewer people, we'll capture that information at check-in.
If I attend Highlander Day, am I committing myself to UCR?
No. Your attendance at Highlander Day has no effect on your status at UCR. It is an opportunity for you to visit the campus, meet faculty, current students, and staff, and lots more.
If I don’t attend Highlander Day, will I lose my offer of admission?
No.
I was admitted to one college, but I'm curious about another. Can I attend different sessions?
Yes, but please note that content will be specific to each college. For example, only majors in the College of Natural and Agricultural Sciences (CNAS) will be discussed at the CNAS Sessions. Also, because UC Riverside is a selective campus and majors are impacted, it is advisable for you to contact your admission counselor as soon as possible to see about changing your major, particularly if you are thinking about moving to a different college.
What happens at Highlander Day?
Highlander Day is a way for you to learn a bit more about the campus, the college you have been admitted to, and life at UC Riverside. If you are unsure about your major, Highlander Day is a great opportunity to explore your options. We have presentations from faculty, staff and students, campus tours, housing tours, department open houses, lunch at the Highlander Union Building, all day entertainment and much, much more planned. For details, please review the agenda as soon as it becomes available.
What is the difference between Discover Day and Highlander Day?

Discover Day, held in the Fall, is our largest open house for students interested in applying and attending UC Riverside. Programming includes application workshops, information sessions about the campus, individual college presentations to help you decide which to apply to, campus tours, housing tours and much more.

Highlander Day, held on April 8th 2017, is our largest open house for recently admitted students for the upcoming Fall term. Programming includes next steps to enrollment, resources available, how to get involved on campus, financial aid, campus tours, and housing tours. The day is based around welcoming you into the Highlander family and showing what life at UC Riverside is all about. Students that are interested in attending UC Riverside for upcoming terms, are welcome to attend as well! There will be special information sessions just for future Highlanders.
