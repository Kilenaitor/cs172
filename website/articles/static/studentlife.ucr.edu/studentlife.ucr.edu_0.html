Student Organization List

 

 

Explore UCR's Student Organizations with the Highlander Link!

Highlander Link is our searchable database of all of our 350+ student organizations and is a great first-place to search when looking for groups to join.

(click the logo above to enter!)

Highlander Link is for use by students, campus staff and faculty, and the general public looking for information about involvement opportunities on campus. Anyone can browse and find out about the organizations on campus, as well descriptions about the organizations and their contact information. Students can express interest and join organizations with the click of a button!

Academic/Professional/Honor Organizations
These groups primary focus is affiliated with an academic department, major, professional preparation or conferring membership as an honor for academic/scholarly/leadership achievement.
Student Life Contact: Gerardo Medina (gerardo.medina@ucr.edu)

Arts and Expressions
Organizations whose primary focus is in the development of, participation in or appreciation of art forms or entertainment or whose focus is to produce one or more publications.
Student Org Network Contact: Geo Mayoral (geovanni.mayoraldavalos@ucr.edu)

Cultural
Organizations whose primary focus seeks to educate and share with the University community on the cultures we have across our campus and around the world.
Student Life Contact: Gerardo Medina (gerardo.medina@ucr.edu)

Fraternity/Sorority
Organizations whose membership is limited to a single gender and granted exception to Title IX requirements for the purpose of cultivating and supporting an environment of brotherhood or sisterhood. In addition, membership in one organization in this category is mutually exclusive to membership in any of the others (i.e., an individual may only belong to one of the organizations in this category).
Student Life Contact: Carly Garcia (carly.garcia@ucr.edu)

Graduate Student Associations
Organizations who are connected with the Graduate Student Association or are mostly/entirely graduate students.
Student Life Contact: Adam Daniels (adam.daniels@ucr.edu)

Action & Awareness
Organizations whose primary focus is on endorsing/supporting candidates for elected positions, ballot initiatives, referenda and the like, and/or debate, discuss, advocate and educate on regional, state, national or international political issues. Action Organizations primary focus is on educating individuals on and engaging in public discussion of issues affecting individuals, society and the environments in which they exist. This group would also includes but not limited to groups focused on social support, topical discussions, gender and/or gender role issues, age related issues who hope to get action on a particular topic.
Student Life Contact: Adam Daniels (adam.daniels@ucr.edu)

Recreational
Organizations whose primary focus is on engaging in recreational activities which may or may not be competitive.
Student Life Contact: Adam Daniels (adam.daniels@ucr.edu)

Service
Organizations whose primary focus is on providing volunteer service to others.
Student Org Network Contact: Joseph Martinez (joseph.martinez@ucr.edu)

Spiritual/Religious/Atheist
Organizations whose primary focus is on educating about and supporting particular spiritual and/or religious practices, philosophies and beliefs.
Student Life Contact: Adam Daniels (adam.daniels@ucr.edu)
