Event Parking is Hiring Students for Winter Quarter 2017

Event parking is now hiring students for Winter Quarter 2017. Please fill out the application on our  Student Employment page. Group interviews will be held the week after Thanksgiving.

Winter Quarter 2017 Gold and Night Commuter Permits

Gold Plus permits have sold out.

Winter Quarter Gold and Night commuter permits are available for purchase online.  A map with Gold permit parking locations is available here.  A $15.00 fee will be charged for lost, stolen or returned permits.

If you select PICKUP as your permit delivery option, your permit may be picked up as follows:

Pickup Schedule

LOT 26
January 9 - January 13
9:00 AM - 1:00 PM

Permits will also be available for pickup the next business day before and after these dates and times at the Parking Services building. Parking Services is located on campus at 683 Linden Street. Office hours are Monday - Friday, 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM.

If you select MAILING for your delivery option, your permit will be mailed on the next business day.  Please allow 7-10 business days for delivery.  The MAILING option is only available through December 15, 2016.  After that date, to ensure your permit is available for the start of the quarter, PICKUP will be the only option. 

2016/ 2017 Campus Apartments / Residence Hall Parking Permits

The online permit purchase window for Housing parking permits has closed.  If you are interested in purchasing a Residence Hall or Glen Mor parking permit, please add your name to the "HOUSING RESIDENT" waiting list via the Parking Account Portal.

If you would like to purchase a Campus Apartment  parking permit for Bannockburn, Falkirk, Oban or University Plaza, you may purchase your permit at the Parking Services office. Parking Services is located on campus at 683 Linden Street. Office hours are Monday - Friday, 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM.

Budget and Planning

The Budget and Planning reports provide data on TAPS' current financial position for 2011/12 and projections through 2021/22.

TAPS Summary of Financial Position 2012/13 - 2017/18

The TAPS Summary of Financial Position 2012/13 - 2017/18 presentation provides highlights of TAPS' 5 year operating plan.  This summary was created in Winter 2013 and while some of the information has changed, it nonetheless provides a good representation of the departments financial position and long range plans. The summary includes background details on TAPS revenue and expenses for 2011/12, projections for 2012/13 and a recovery and growth plan for 2014/15 through 2017/18.

Long Range Parking Model

The TAPS Long Range Parking Model provides an operating plan and budget through 2021/22 (updated in Spring 2014) and consists of 5 major components:

Projected Operating Income
Projected Operating Expenses
Parking Lot Renewal and Replacement Projects
Equipment Replacement
Parking Space Supply and Demand

Detailed Operating Budget Information

TAPS' operating budget is divided into three distinct activities:  Permits, Fines and Forfeitures, and Transit Operations.  Each  year TAPS submits its budget proposal to the Office of Resource Planning and Budget and ultimately to the EVC/Provost.  Included below are the selected tables for the operating budgets submitted and approved by the EVC/Provost for fiscal year 2014/15.  Information from these tables are combined to perform the revenue and expenditure calculations used in the long range parking model. About Us

UCR's Parking Services department is committed to providing innovative and sustainable solutions to efficiently meet traffic and parking demands. Each day, UCR accommodates thousands of students, faculty and staff commuting to campus. Our goal is to get you here safely and efficiently.
