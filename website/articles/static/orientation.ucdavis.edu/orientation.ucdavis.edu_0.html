Welcome to UC Davis!

Register

Register for Orientation through MyAdmissions. Transfer registration closes on June 6, 2016. First-Year registration closes two weeks (14 days) before the registered session. International and National registration closes on July 1, 2016.

Permit 24-48 business hours after submitting a Statement of Intent to Register (SIR) for the Orientation Registration system to update before registering. 

Announcements

The Orientation Program office is closed on weekends (Saturdays and Sundays), please allow 48-72 hours for a response to any messages.

Congratulations on your admission into one of the most innovative research institutions in the country! The Division of Student Affairs and the entire UC Davis campus welcome you, our newest scholars and family members, into UC Davis.

Orientation is a critical academic program that should not be missed! Every new student is expected to participate in the Orientation program. Orientation is your opportunity to learn how to navigate campus and academic resources. During Orientation you will interact with students, staff and faculty from diverse backgrounds who will share with you strategies for academic, personal and global success. We are excited to help you create a home away from home in partnership with your family.

The goals of the UC Davis Orientation program are to:

  • Support your transition into a large research university;
  • Connect you with your college and academic advising resources, including undergraduate research and internship opportunities;
  • Help you understand what it means to be a respected and respectful member of our multicultural UC Davis community by introducing you to our Principles of Community;
  • Encourage you to engage with the campus community and get involved in various clubs and organizations;
  • Guide you in finding the answers to your important questions.

In essence, we believe that the key to being successful at UC Davis begins with Orientation and quality advising. We look forward to helping students and their families embark on this new journey. Orientation is a once-in-a-lifetime experience where you will chart your academic course, meet new people, discover mentors and caring advisers, begin to explore your true passions, and of course, have fun. It’s the beginning of developing your Aggie Pride, and we are thrilled to welcome you this summer!
